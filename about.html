Bio

The Intel 80386, also known as i386 or just 386 was my first ever computer. It was a 32-bit microprocessor which was first introduced in 1985. By the time my family got hold of one however it was 1996 and I was 12 years old.

I had owned games consoles such as the "NES" (Nintendo Entertainment System) and the Sega Mega Drive (or Genesis if your from the U.S.). This was our first home computer however and it ran Windows 3.1 operating system.

My passion for all things technical began here. I was engroced with the capabilities of the machine we now owned. Gaming, word processing, image processing, the possibilities were endless.

At around age 14 I was competing in the competitive online gaming community playing titles such as Doom, Quake and Unreal (I had upgraded my system entirely by this time of course.) I was soon joining clans and teams and I began being interested in web design

I began learning HTML and back then used Dreamweaver to build websites. I have come a long way since those early days. For a while I had strayed from my passion of web design as I began college and excessive alcohol consumption along with it.

Recently I have rediscovered my passion for web design and development and have worked hard to catch up with the modern day technologies and standards
